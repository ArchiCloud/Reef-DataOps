Monitoring Methods


Monitoring coral fragments is a crucial aspect of assessing their survivorship and overall health status. The primary objective of this monitoring effort is to swiftly evaluate the progress and effectiveness of the restoration project, identify any potential issues, and ensure the long-term sustainability of the rehabilitated coral reef habitat.


Survivorship and Health Status Recording for the Coral Fragments

During the monitoring surveys, the health status of the coral fragments was carefully observed and documented. Any abnormalities, such as coral bleaching, tissue necrosis, or signs of stress, were noted. These records are vital in identifying potential threats to the transplanted corals and understanding the factors that may affect their health and resilience. To assess the survivorship of the transplanted coral fragments, monitoring teams conducted visual inspections on each reef tile during the quarterly surveys.

The Abundance and Diversity of Indicator Species (Fish and Invertebrates)

In addition to monitoring the coral fragments, the study also focused on assessing the abundance and diversity of specific indicator species, namely fish and invertebrates, as designated by Reef Check (Arabian Gulf). These indicator species are representative of the overall health and ecological balance of the marine environment. The data collected on the abundance and diversity of these indicator species were recorded, providing a comprehensive overview of the local marine ecosystem's health.

This survey will be conducted at both a restoration site, and a control site, with similar habitat and depth in proximity to the restoration site (around 20m away from the restoration site). The list of indicator species specifically designed for the Arabian Gulf by Reef Check was adopted and included in this study. The species on this list are known to be particularly sensitive to changes in their environment, making them excellent indicators of the overall ecological status. By employing this standardized list, the study ensured that the findings could be compared with other research efforts in the region, promoting broader scientific understanding and conservation efforts.

Abnormality Detection and Reporting

In the event of any coral detachment, or notable changes in the structural formation of the deployment, the team will report and take immediate mitigation measures, such as re-attaching the coral fragments to the Reef Tiles. This ensures immediate attention and appropriate action to address any issues that may arise during the restoration process.
